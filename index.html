01 · Case Study PlanYear LLC · B2B SaaS

Consultation Booking — Zero to One

Transforming messy, manual scheduling into an automated, timezone-aware booking system for US benefits consultants and employees.

Problem
Manual scheduling causing missed requests & timezone chaos
Approach
Discovery & scoping · Vendor evaluation · Cross-functional delivery
Outcome
Eliminated manual scheduling — automated, timezone-aware, zero back-and-forth
Role
Product Manager · Full ownership from discovery to launch
Read case study
Background & Why Now

The platform's existing scheduling capability had grown beyond what it was originally designed to handle. Consultants were managing an increasing volume of booking requests manually, and the system could not accommodate multi-timezone scheduling or distribute workload across a team automatically — everything funnelled through a single point of contact who then reassigned calls by hand.

Usage patterns and direct consultant feedback confirmed that scheduling was among the most heavily used features during the annual renewal period — typically the start of the year. Shipping this feature before renewals began was a clear, time-bound priority.

User Personas · Problem Space

The following personas are representative composites drawn from direct conversations with benefits consultants during the discovery process.

Benefits Consultant · Detroit

"I get many consultation requests by email, and after holidays my inbox is overwhelming. Auto-replies help temporarily, but they don't provide a good experience for clients."

Lead Benefits Consultant · San Mateo

"With clients across time zones, some calls get booked at really odd hours. I spend hours reassigning them to my team — I want more time growing my team, not scheduling calls."

Employee · San Mateo

"Booking appointments with the brokerage takes too long. I'm sometimes unsure if I'm even contacting the right person about my benefits."

My Approach
1
Requirement AnalysisCollaborated with design, engineering, and QA leads to define the problem precisely before writing a single requirement — clarifying consultant routing logic, feature placement, and availability management. Used MoSCoW to prioritise scope.
2
Build vs. Buy EvaluationAssessed cost, time, and resource impact of in-house vs. third-party. Evaluated the existing scheduling integration alongside other providers, assessing each against the hard requirements: round-robin assignment, multi-resource booking, custom availability rules, and multi-timezone support. The existing solution did not meet all requirements, so a broader market evaluation followed.
3
OnSched SelectedArranged a live demo and sandbox access to validate the APIs hands-on before committing. OnSched's round-robin logic, multi-resource booking, custom availability, and timezone handling covered every requirement. Their responsiveness during evaluation was also a signal of the partnership to come.
4
API Architecture DecisionThe selected API used a hierarchical data model that needed to be mapped thoughtfully onto the platform's multi-tenant structure — spanning environments, client organisations, and individual consultant profiles. I designed the mapping to ensure clean data isolation and correct routing across the platform, a decision that shaped how the integration scaled.
5
Cross-Functional Delivery & Vendor CollaborationCollaborated with design on wireframes and supported engineering through sprints. When API defects emerged during development, I escalated directly to the vendor — joining calls with their engineering team to resolve issues in real time. Several bugs were fixed on the spot, keeping delivery on track without workarounds accumulating in the codebase.
The Solution
For Consultants — BenAdmin Portal
  • Create profiles with timezone, availability hours, and assigned employer groups
  • Scheduling APIs triggered on save — profiles maintained automatically
  • Calendar sync enables real-time availability management
  • Mark unavailability in advance — no requests slip through on holidays
For Employees — BenGuides Portal
  • One-click "Benefits Consultation" entry point in the BenGuide
  • Automatic timezone matching and availability checking
  • Balanced round-robin assignment ensures even consultant workload
  • Automated confirmation emails sent on booking
Eliminated hours of weekly manual reassignment — lead consultants freed to grow their teams, not manage calendars
Cross-timezone scheduling fully automated — zero manual intervention across distributed consultant teams
Employees routed to the right consultant automatically — no uncertainty, no wrong-person emails
Delivered before the annual renewal period — the highest-traffic window — meeting the strategic deadline
What I'd Do Differently

Looking back, I was managing multiple features in parallel and the sequencing could have been sharper. Prioritising the features in a different order would have given each more breathing room and sharper focus during delivery. This shaped how I approach roadmap sequencing: I now ask not just "what's most needed?" but "what order creates the most space for the team to do their best work?" — and I weight strategic sequencing more explicitly when building roadmaps.
